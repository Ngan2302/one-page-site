Impact of Bushfire in Australia


Bushfire is not a strange natural disaster in Australia It is always said to be uncontrolled and unpredictable. Every years, bushfire have not just killed so many people but also highly damaged to our nature, killed the environment.However, not everyone knows what bushfire really is and how to deal with that. In this site, you can increase your knowledge about this topic.



What bushfire really is?

1 / 3
Bushfire is a type of wildfire, it burns through areas of bushland, including forest, savannahs or grassland. They are always widespread, unpredictable and hard to control. Because of Australia’s dry environment and large amount of bush, bushfire is very common here.
2 / 3
Bushfires are a result of climate change. Because of the heat of the earth and dryness of the environment, fire is easy to appear on some fire prone and combustible plants, especially some kind of Australian native plant. However, bushfires are sometimes also caused by humans. Some people, because of their lack of knowledge or personal benefits, burnt the forest to clear land or agriculture or trading purposes.
3 / 3
Bushfires affect not just the environment but also humans and animals. Bushfires can create air pollution, affect to human’s health and quality of life. Bushfires can increase the temperature and create drought. Bushfires can decrease the amount of animal and plant species, reduce the abundance of the environment. Beside this, bushfire can directly kill animals and even humans, not just by the fire but also its smoke.

- Also known as Victorian bushfire

- 2 millions hectares were burnt

So what should you do?


To handle in bushfire

Keep an eye on conditions outside. You may become aware of a fire before the emergency services.
Follow your plan. If you don't have a bushfire survival plan ready, you should leave early. Always let family and friends know what you're doing.
Wear protective clothing.Wear loose-fitting clothing made from natural fibres like wool, denim or heavy cotton and sturdy boots.
Stay informed. Find out if there's a fire near you
Protect yourself first. You can use protective clothes or a wet tissue to cover your nose and mouth.

To prepare for bushfire

Make a planTalk with your household about what you will do if there's a fire in your area, and make a written plan. You should make decisions like where you will go and what your trigger to leave will be.
Fire danger ratings Know what each fire danger rating means and use them as your sign to stay or leave. Never wait and see. On days forecast for catastrophic or extreme fire danger, your safest option is to leave early, either the day before, or very early in the morning.
Know your 'Safer Place' If you live in or intend to travel to a high-risk bushfire area, know the location of your nearest Neighbourhood Safer Place, meeting point or evacuation centre and how to get there. Neighbourhood Safer Places are places of last resort; they may save your life, but they do not guarantee safety.
Prepare your survival kit Be sure your bushfire survival kit is ready and you know where it is.

To take part in reducing bushfire

Clean the bushes in your area regularly.Create empty space between shrubs and trees to reduce the chances of flames. Mop grassy areas and leaves regularly.
Do not use fireworks in the area near vegetation.Use them in watery areas such as pools or rivers. Check your local ordinances to verify the restrictions and make the right move when using fireworks.
Throw trash in the right place.Because plastic and wood will make bushes even hotter, it can increase the percentage of fire and also pollute the environment.
Never leave a fire unattended. If you finish camping, always put off the campfire and never it or if you finish smoking, always put off the fire before throwing it away.
